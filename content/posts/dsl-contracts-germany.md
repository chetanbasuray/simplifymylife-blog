German DSL contracts layer speeds, regional availability, and countless promo credits into dense German-language PDFs. This guide keeps everything in plain English while showing you how to use the CHECK24 DSL calculator so you land on a reliable connection without wasting your weekend.

### Why review DSL tariffs regularly

Providers refresh incentives every quarter. If you stay on a legacy plan you miss out on lower base fees and modern hardware bundles. Running a comparison once a year helps you:

- Confirm whether fiber or VDSL upgrades reached your address.
- Lock in introductory discounts before they expire.
- Track hidden activation or shipping fees before they hit your first invoice.

### Gather data before opening CHECK24

Having a few details ready keeps the widget painless. Jot down:

1. **Postal code and street number** – Availability depends on the local Telekom line, so precision matters.
2. **Desired download/upload speeds** – Know the minimum Mbps your household needs for video calls or gaming.
3. **Current contract end date** – You usually need to cancel 1–3 months in advance.
4. **Hardware preference** – Decide if you need a provider router, will bring your own, or want Wi-Fi mesh add-ons.

### Step-by-step DSL switching workflow

Stay organized with these guardrails:

- Save screenshots of your current router login, contract PDFs, and Kündigung confirmations in a shared folder.
- Create a recurring task titled “DSL renewal” that fires 120 days before your minimum term ends.
- Turn on CHECK24 alerts so you know when new fiber-ready deals land in your city.

1. **Collect documents** – Grab your latest invoice, customer number, and DSL line ID if available.

### Keep life simple after the switch

- **Create calendar reminders** for the activation day and for 60/30 days before the next renewal window.
- **Store every document** (router shipping notices, SEPA mandates, data protection forms) in a shared cloud folder.
- **Monitor performance** by logging speed tests quarterly. If actual speeds drop 15% below the contract, rerun the calculator mid-term.
